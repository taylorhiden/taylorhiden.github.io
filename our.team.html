Nathan Parsons-Schwarz


Executive Vice President

@ParsonsSchwarz

Nathan has over seven years of experience working as a field operative in ten states, including work for the Republican Parties of Virginia, Arkansas and Pennsylvania. From 2006 to 2008, he served as Special Assistant to Kentucky Secretary of State Trey Grayson, where, among other duties, he coordinated the Secretary’s Civics Initiative. In 2005, Parsons-Schwarz led the efforts of the College Republican National Committee in New Jersey, where he started or strengthened over a dozen chapter throughout the state. He also has extensive experience in volunteer recruitment, absentee and early voting procedures and direct mail.

Torrey Shearer


Senior Director


Named by the D.C. GOP as one of the Most Influential Republicans Under 35, Torrey provides years of public affairs experience lobbying before the U.S. Congress and delivers a comprehensive understanding of the legislative process through analysis of complex and rapidly evolving policy issues. Prior to joining Allegiance, he was a public affairs advisor in the Government Section at Holland & Knight and represented a broad portfolio of corporate, association and tribal government clients. Prior to joining Holland & Knight, Torrey became one of the first members of a team selected by T. Boone Pickens to organize and run a $50 million campaign to turn America’s attention to reducing dependence on foreign oil. Prior to joining the Pickens Plan, he served as a legislative assistant in the federal government affairs office of BNSF Railway Company. He is actively engaged in the national political process and has worked on numerous campaigns at the state and federal level.

Emily Harlan


Deputy Director of Development


Emily develops and implements strategies for corporations to harness their reputational, financial and lobbying resources to help advance non-discrimination legislation at the state and federal levels. Previously, Emily served as the Administrative and Policy Associate for American Unity Fund and has interned for Speaker John Boehner and Congressman Todd Platts of Pennsylvania. Emily graduated from Washington and Lee University with a double major in Economics and Politics.

Erika Scheffer


Director, State Affairs


Erika oversees advocacy campaigns and policy development across all fifty states. Erika works with elected officials, strategic partners, and major donors to advance causes with conservative support. Erika’s experiences include holding multiple positions over the course of seven and a half years at the National Rifle Association, where she was most recently Deputy Director of Political Affairs at the NRA – Office of Advancement. Previously, Erika also worked for Fairfax County Office of Public Affairs and interned on Capitol Hill for Congressman Bill Shuster (PA-9). Erika earned a Master of Public Policy and a Bachelor of Arts in Government and International Politics from George Mason University.

Keeley Hanlon


Director


Keeley leads Allegiance’s political accountability and public education efforts in the states and at the federal level, including mobilizing center-right constituencies and advancing conservative support across the country for our causes. Before working for Allegiance, Keeley spent three election cycles managing voter outreach, political advertising, and grassroots engagement as part of the political team at the National Federation of Independent Business and went on to manage national field operations at the ONE Campaign. She has assisted with several US Senate and Congressional races and was a part of Wisconsin Governor Scott Walker’s successful effort in the 2012 recall election.

Mike Palicz

Taylor Hiden


Digital Associate

Christine Baratta


Communications Director


Christine Baratta is a communications and media specialist with 16 years of experience in broadcasting, politics and public relations. She has worked as a talk radio producer in Boston for over 10 years and was an executive producer at Bloomberg LP, the news leader in global business and financial information. While at Bloomberg, she covered the 2008 New Hampshire First-in-the-Nation Primary and Presidential Election and was responsible for producing a daily live national stock market and political radio show on Bloomberg Radio/WBBR (1130AM). Christine’s political experience includes serving as the Communications Director for the New Hampshire Republican Party and as the Communications Director for multiple electoral and issue advocacy campaigns.
